Your obligations
Four layers of compliance — all active, all enforceable
Florida FIPA
Applies to any entity that stores, uses, or maintains personal information. Breach notification required within 30 days. Florida Attorney General notification required when 500+ individuals are affected. Your 44,000 records qualify immediately.
Penalties per violation — no cap
FREC / Chapter 475
The Florida Real Estate Commission requires compliance with data privacy laws including FIPA as part of licensed brokerage operations. Failure to protect client data can result in formal disciplinary action including license suspension or revocation.
Real estate license at risk
Century 21 Franchise Agreement
Your FDD mandates encryption, authentication controls, minimum $500K cyber insurance, and P&P Manual compliance. Your agents' acts and omissions fall under your indemnification obligation to Century 21 corporate — their mistakes are your liability.
Franchise termination grounds
FTC Safeguards / GLBA
Applies where financial data flows through your environment. Requires a written WISP, designated Qualified Individual, annual penetration testing, and FTC breach notification within 30 days at 500+ affected consumers. That notification becomes public record.
Up to $51,744 per violation per day

⚠  Scope Clarification

This program addresses your Cyber Liability Defense layer exclusively — the documentation, policies, evidence collection, compliance mapping, and enforcement infrastructure that protects you legally and keeps your insurance claim defensible.

The technical remediation items identified in the pen test — password hygiene, drive encryption, patch management, domain admin controls, and endpoint security — are the responsibility of your existing IT and security team. MOSTRO's role in this area is advisory only. We identify the gaps, prioritize them by risk and compliance impact, and provide your team with clear direction on what needs to be fixed and why. We do not perform technical remediation.

Think of it this way: MOSTRO builds and manages the compliance program. Your IT team fixes the technical problems. Both are necessary. They are not the same job.

For organizations that want MOSTRO to go beyond the compliance layer, technical remediation is available as a separate engagement. In this capacity, MOSTRO works directly alongside your existing IT team — not replacing them, but coordinating, directing, and executing on the technical gaps identified in the pen test. Scope, timeline, and investment for this engagement are defined separately based on your specific environment and priorities.
